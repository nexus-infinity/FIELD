If someone keeps promising to fix all your problems but asks for more payments, it is likely a scam. Scammers often use tactics like this to exploit people's vulnerabilities and extract more money from them. Here's what you can do in such a situation:

1. Stop making payments: Do not make any more payments to the person or organization promising to fix your problems. Continuing to pay them will only prolong the scam and potentially result in further financial loss.

2. Cut off contact: Cease all communication with the scammer. Block their phone number, email address, and any other means of contact they have used to reach you. Do not engage with them any further.

3. Report the scam: Report the scam to your local law enforcement agency and provide them with all the details of the scam, including any communication or evidence you have. This will help authorities take action against the scammer and prevent them from targeting others.

4. Notify your bank or credit card company: If you made payments to the scammer using your bank account or credit card, contact your financial institution immediately. Inform them about the scam and ask them to freeze your accounts or credit cards to prevent further unauthorized transactions.

5. Seek legal advice: If you have suffered significant financial loss or believe you may have legal recourse, consider consulting with a lawyer who specializes in fraud or consumer protection. They can provide guidance on your options and help you navigate the legal process if necessary.

Remember, it's important to be cautious and skeptical when dealing with anyone who promises to fix all your problems for a fee. Legitimate professionals and service providers will not ask for repeated payments without delivering the promised results. Trust your instincts and take steps to protect yourself from scams.
